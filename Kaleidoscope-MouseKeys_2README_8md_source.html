12 Have you ever wanted to control the mouse cursor from the comfort of your
13 keyboard? With this plugin, you can. While it may not replace the mouse in all
14 situations, there are plenty of cases where one will not have to lift their
15 hands off the keyboard just to nudge the mouse cursor away a little.
16 
17 Of course, there are a lot more one can do with the plugin than to nudge the
18 cursor! Mouse keys are provided for all four *and* diagonal movement; mouse
19 buttons; and a unique warping mechanism too. And not only these: the speed of
20 the cursor, the mouse wheel, and that of acceleration can all be configured to
21 match one's desired behaviour.
22 
23 ## Using the plugin
24 
25 To use the plugin, simply include the header in your Sketch, tell the firmware
26 to use the `MouseKeys` object, and place mouse keys on your keymap. It is best
27 illustrated with an example:
28 
29 ```c++
30 #include <Kaleidoscope.h>
31 #include <Kaleidoscope-MouseKeys.h>
32 
33 // Somewhere in the keymap:
34 Key_mouseUp, Key_mouseDn, Key_mouseL, Key_mouseR,
35 Key_mouseBtnL, Key_mouseBtnR
36 
37 void setup() {
38  Kaleidoscope.use(&MouseKeys);
39 
40  Kaleidoscope.setup ();
41 }
42 ```

44 ## Keys provided by the plugin
45 
46 The plugin provides a number of keys one can put on the keymap, that allow
47 control of the mouse. They can be divided into a few groups:
48 
49 ### Cursor movement
50 
51 The simplest set of keys are the mouse cursor movement keys. These move the
52 cursor one direction or the other, with speed and acceleration factored in. When
53 a mouse cursor movement key is held down, it will move `.speed` pixels each
54 `.speedDelay` milliseconds without acceleration. But when `.accelSpeed` is
55 non-zero (and it is not zero by default,
56 see [below](#accelspeed-and-acceldelay)), the speed will increase by
57 `.accelSpeed` every `.accelDelay` milliseconds. Thus, unless configured
58 otherwise, holding a direction will move that way at increasing speed.
59 
60 One can hold more than one key down at the same time, and the cursor will move
61 towards a direction that is the combination of the keys held. For example,
62 holding the "mouse up" and "mouse right" keys together will move the cursor
63 diagonally up and right.
64 
65 The cursor movement keys are as follows:
66 
67 * `Key_mouseUp`, `Key_mouseDn`, `Key_mouseL`, `Key_mouseR`: Move the cursor up,
68  down, left, or right, respectively.
69 * `Key_mouseUpL`, `Key_mouseUpR`, `Key_mouseDnL`, `Key_mouseDnR`: Move the
70  cursor up-left, up-right, down-left, down-right, respectively.
71 
72 ### Scroll wheel
73 
74 Controlling the scroll wheel is similarly simple. It does not have acceleration,
75 but one can control the speed with the `.wheelSpeed` and `.wheelDelay`
76 properties (see below).
77 
78 * `Key_mouseScrollUp`, `Key_mouseScrollDn`: Scroll the mouse wheel up or down,
79  respectively.
80 * `Key_mouseScrollL`, `Key_mouseScrollR`: Scroll the mouse wheel left or right,
81  respectively.
82 
83 ### Buttons
84 
85 Buttons are even simpler than movement: there is no movement speed, nor
86 acceleration involved. One just presses them.
87 
88 * `Key_mouseBtnL`, `Key_mouseBtnM`, `Key_mouseBtnR`: The left, middle, and right
89  mouse buttons, respectively.
90 
91 ### Warping
92 
93 Warping is one of the most interesting features of the plugin, and is a feature
94 unique to Kaleidoscope, as far as we can tell. The warping keys position the
95 mouse cursor within a quadrant of the screen on first press, and any subsequent
96 taps will warp within the previously selected quadrant. For example, pressing
97 the north-west warp key twice will first jump to the middle of the north-west
98 quadrant of your screen, then select the north-west quadrant of that, and jump
99 to the middle of it.
100 
101 To stop warping, use any other mouse key, or hit the "warp end" key.
102 
103 This features works out of the box on Windows and OSX. On Linux, a patch exists,
104 but has not been finalised and merged yet.
105 
106 The warping keys are the following:
107 
108 * `Key_mouseWarpNW`, `Key_mouseWarpNE`, `Key_mouseWarpSW`, `Key_mouseWarpSE`:
109  Warp towards the north-west, north-east, south-west, or south-east quadrants,
110  respectively.
111 * `Key_mouseWarpEnd`: End the warping sequence, resetting it to the default
112  state. Using any of the warping keys after this will start from the whole
113  screen again.

115 ## Plugin methods
116 
117 The plugin provides a `MouseKeys` object, with the following methods and
118 properties available:
119 
120 ### `.speed` and `.speedDelay`
121 
122 > These two control the speed of the mouse cursor, when a movement key is held.
123 > The former, `.speed`, controls the amount of pixels the cursor moves, when it
124 > has to move, and defaults to 1. The latter, `.speedDelay` is the amount of
125 > time - in milliseconds - to wait between two movements, and defaults to 0, no
126 > delay.
127 
128 ### `.accelSpeed` and `.accelDelay`
129 
130 > These two properties control the speed of acceleration. The former,
131 > `.accelSpeed`, controls how much the speed shall be increased at each step,
132 > while the second, `.accelDelay`, controls how often (in milliseconds)
133 > acceleration should be applied.
134 >
135 > They default to 1 pixel and 50 milliseconds, respectively.
136 
137 ### `.wheelSpeed` and `.wheelDelay`
138 
139 > The last two properties supported by the plugin control the mouse wheel
140 > scrolling speed. The former, `.wheelSpeed`, controls the amount of ticks the
141 > wheel shall scroll, and defaults to 1. The second, `.wheelDelay`, controls the
142 > delay between two scroll events, and defaults to 50 milliseconds.
